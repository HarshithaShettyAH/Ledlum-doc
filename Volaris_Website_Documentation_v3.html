The Core Insight for Volaris

Fanzart — the market leader with no e-commerce at all — proves that a designer fan brand's website exists to build desire and direct footfall to showrooms, not to process transactions. Anemos has the best page structure but zero identity. Luft has personality but no depth. Volaris's opening is to bring all three strengths together: Fanzart's brand confidence, Anemos's information depth, and a visual system that outclasses all of them.

LONDON DESIGN STUDIO

Layer Design

Industrial product design · London · Bang & Olufsen, Nike, Ledger

Pure-black site with a single five-word headline. No decorative elements — project photography fills the screen and does all the work. Impact statistics (150+ collaborations, 200+ awards) are placed prominently as proof, not hidden. Nav has exactly six links.

What Volaris borrows:

  • Lead with credibility numbers where appropriate
  • Full-bleed product photography carries more weight than any copy
  • Navigation stripped to only what is truly necessary
  • Deep negative space signals confidence, not emptiness

LIFESTYLE MAGAZINE

Joshua's Magazine

Luxury lifestyle magazine · Automotive, design, style, food

Opens with a quoted philosophy — unexpected, memorable. Warm brown-black tones, never cold. The homepage scrolls like a magazine spread rather than navigating like a website. Large editorial image tiles anchor each feature. Newsletter sits quietly at the bottom — no pop-ups.

What Volaris borrows:

  • Open with a brand philosophy statement, not a product grid
  • Warm brown-blacks feel inviting — cold greys feel clinical
  • The page should reward slow scrolling, like reading a magazine
  • Large image tiles with minimal text overlay — image does the work
  • Email capture is quiet and respectful — never a pop-up
Studio DADO
HOSPITALITY DESIGN

Studio Dado

Interior design for hospitality · Miami · "Form Follows Feeling"

Warm cream background — the opposite of cold tech white. Opens with a philosophy headline before showing a single project. Autoplay looping video in the hero, muted. Project cards show the space in full-bleed photography with only a name and category. Footer carries just an address, email, and phone — nothing more.

What Volaris borrows:

  • Warm cream/ivory backgrounds — premium without being aggressive
  • Lead with brand philosophy before revealing any product
  • Autoplay looping video creates atmosphere without demanding attention
  • Project cards: full image, minimal text — the room sells itself
  • Minimal footer with physical address builds real-world trust

Volaris Aesthetic Direction — The Synthesis

Layer's discipline tells us: pick one headline, make it count, let silence do the rest. Joshua's warmth tells us: use brown-blacks, not cold greys — warmth invites while cool distances. Studio Dado's philosophy-first approach tells us: before showing a single fan, tell the visitor how the room should feel. Together, these three form a clear brief: a luxury Indian fan brand whose website reads like a design atelier, not an appliance catalogue.

What Volaris Does Differently
Four strategic advantages that separate Volaris from every competitor in the Indian market

The Catalogue as Primary CTA

While competitors end pages with "Add to Cart" or "Enquire Now," every Volaris page ends with "Download Catalogue." A beautifully produced PDF is a leave-behind that lives on desks, gets shown to interior designers, and resurfaces weeks later when the buyer is ready. It is a sales tool that keeps working long after the session ends.

CMM as the Brand Metric

Luft built AIRFEEL™ as their ownable metric. Volaris has something better already in the catalogue: CMM — cubic metres per minute (and its CFM equivalent). Every product page in the catalogue lists both values — Luna at 364 m³/min (13,000 CFM), Clove at 275 m³/min (9,821 CFM), Trident at 210 m³/min (7,500 CFM). Displayed as a visual airflow bar on the website, this turns a dry engineering number into a powerful story about how much air actually moves through a room. The catalogue also teaches visitors why RPM alone is misleading — a lesson Volaris owns online.

The House of Ledlum Story

Fanzart is a product brand. Anemos is a multi-brand store. Volaris carries the backing of the House of Ledlum — Ledlum Lighting Solutions LLP, founded 2017, with sister brands Aerglo (decorative lighting), Artizan (architectural projects since 2020), and Astara (commercial LED). This is a lineage story none of the competitors can replicate. It elevates Volaris from a product to a design house, the way an Italian furniture brand references the family behind the craft.

Editorial Over Commercial

All three competitors either look like Shopify stores or generic product catalogues. Volaris will present itself as an interior design publication. Room photography leads. Specifications follow. Prices never appear. The website creates desire — the dealer closes the sale.

The Fan Selector — Room-Size Intelligence

The catalogue already contains a "How to Find the Perfect Fan?" guide — room diagrams with size recommendations. Volaris brings this to life as an interactive fan selector: choose your room type, get the right fan size. No competitor offers this level of guided education. The selector also teaches the critical difference between RPM and CMM/CFM — a concept Volaris owns online.

Multi-Size Design Families

Many Volaris designs come in multiple sizes with unique model codes — Venus alone spans 24"/36"/42"/52" across four SKUs (VFR003–006), each with different CMM and wattage ratings. The product page must handle size variants elegantly: one design, multiple sizes, distinct airflow data per size. This is more sophisticated than any competitor's product architecture.

📌 Product Detail — Critical Design Decisions

  • URL is /designs/luna — not /products/. The language signals a design studio, not a store
  • No price shown anywhere — ever. Enquire or find a dealer. This protects dealer relationships and reinforces the brand tier
  • CMM Airflow Bar — Volaris's branded performance metric. Turns a dry engineering figure into a visual story about air and space
  • Primary CTA: "Enquire About This Design" — warm and considered language. Not transactional
  • Secondary CTA: "Download Catalogue" — the leave-behind is always one click away

The Catalogue-First Philosophy
Why Download Catalogue is the most powerful CTA a premium Indian fan brand can use

12.3 — RPM vs CMM/CFM Education

Airflow Education — A Key SEO and Brand Differentiator

The catalogue dedicates a full page to explaining "RPM vs CFM" — a topic no competitor covers online. This becomes a dedicated page (or section) on the Volaris website, owning the search query "what is CMM in ceiling fans India." Key insights from the catalogue to feature:

  • Factors affecting airflow: Blade shape, material weight, fan size, ceiling height, and rod length
  • Material matters: ABS, Plywood, and Polycarbonate produce more airflow than heavier Solid Wood — recommend timber fans for cooler cities or AC rooms
  • RPM ≠ Airflow: "A slow RPM fan can still have high CFM" — RPM measures rotation speed, CMM/CFM measures actual air volume moved
  • CMM is the metric that matters: Cubic metres per minute tells you how much air your room actually receives
  • Ceiling height rule: Ceilings over 12ft require the correct rod size or airflow is reduced
